Microsoft Business School Technical Elite Workshops

​​Starting from the beginning of 2015, Microsoft Business School will be holding a series of technical elite workshops. Each month we will provide technical training for different products.

2015-1​

Starting from the beginning of 2015, Microsoft Business School will be holding a series of technical elite workshops. Each month we will provide technical training for different products.
In early February, Microsoft Business School successfully held the Windows Azure hands-on lab. This took trainees step-by-step through the Windows Azure Cloud World, providing an in-depth explanation of the technology completely in Chinese so they can understand the latest development of the technology in the rest of the world. Trainees received an in-depth explanation of Windows Azure features and details from tech advisors in Microsoft’s Global Partner Service division. The content includes how to achieve fast, flexible, and cost-effective services related to networks, authentication, websites, databases, and others in the next-generation IDC. In addition, the instructors gave the trainees a hands-on experience of the Chinese test environment so they can truly experience the cloud technology and understand its value.
Intended Audience
Enterprise architects, technical engineers, and developers with IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service) and PaaS (Platform as a Service) project experience and no years of experience requirement. They should also possess the following:
- Network construction, Hyper-V, high availability, and datacenter project experience
- An understanding of Web Service concepts, preferably with the relevant development experience
- A basic knowledge of SQL Server and SharePoint
Training Benefits
- Trainees have understood the latest Windows Azure functions and features, how to migrate from traditional datacenter services to the Windows Azure cloud datacenter, and the matching relationship between various services and the Windows Azure Cloud
Services
- Trainees have had an in-depth understanding of the next-generation IDC, including how to implement services related to networks, authentication, websites, databases, and others in the cloud.

Azure University Partner Training

​​The Azure University Partner Training Course – Technical Training was held in Shanghai from October 23rd to 24th. The course introduces Azure’s features and architecture so partners can learn how to quickly develop cloud computing applications on Azure.

2014-10​

The Azure University Partner Training Course – Technical Training was held in Shanghai from October 23rd to 24th. The course introduces Azure’s features and architecture so partners can learn how to quickly develop cloud computing applications on Azure, e.g. development testing, SQL servers, SharePoint servers, script-based daily monitoring and operation of Azure resources. Besides invited experienced industry leaders as trainers, this event gave participants the chance to use the Azure platform themselves, interact with industry elites, and share experiences.
