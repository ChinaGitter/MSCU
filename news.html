Microsoft China University H2 offsite

Microsoft China University H2 offsite was held on Wednesday May 28 in a camping restaurant. 20+ colleagues from Academies, HR, Tele team joined this offsite. MSCU offsite is scheduled twice a year and is an opportunity of all L&D teams gathering together and sharing findings and learnings.

2014-5​

Microsoft China University H2 offsite was held on Wednesday May 28 in a camping restaurant. 20+ colleagues from Academies, HR, Tele team joined this offsite. MSCU offsite is scheduled twice a year and is an opportunity of all L&D teams gathering together and sharing findings and learnings.

In the morning, Flora and Xiaoming from Tele team brought an ice-breaking game and all people had fun with it. After Sayta’s video on “Culture Change”, which is the theme of this offsite as well, Yan Li from Leadership academy organized a discussion on “What’s the aspect most related/interested you from the aspects of the New culture, which includes Build on others, Be a challenger, Obsess about customers, Optimistic realism, Be a learner, Be great, Adapt to changing environment, and Reflect on Microsoft unique strength”. All participates selected an aspect which they thought most important and gave their arguments. Then people divided into 3 groups and worked on how to facilitate/build on this aspect of culture change as a learning organization in depth.

After lunch, the afternoon event began with a fun activity organized by Mike Emery, 4 groups of people discussed on FY15 readiness team goals and described their goals with photos only. Followed this game, Oliver from Engineering Academy introduced a popular yearly event “Hackathon”, which became a hot topic after Sayta’s speaking on a cultural shift to a Growth Hacking Culture. 

Though temperature was high, all attendees showed great interest in the topics and actively joined the discussion. In the end, 3 virtual teams separately demonstrated their detailed actions plans on how to facilitate/build on the culture change. To follow the MSCU offsite practice, the 3 virtual teams will schedule regular meetings to work on this plan and implement it as a project in FY15. That’s the way we as a learning organization to make business impacts and influence the culture change in a long run.

The HKUST Business School is young, dynamic and very well respected for the quality of its programs and the impact of its research. Reflecting the School's position at the forefront of business education, all full-time faculty hold PhDs and adjunct faculty are drawn from among the senior leaders of major corporations around the world.

2013-9​

The Mirosoft Campus Day pilot program was successfully implemented on September 16 at Beijing Post & Telecommunications University, with an aim of promotion Microsoft’s technology, culture, and products among students and leverage this to recruit talent. By offering product showcases, alumni sharing, demos, and mock interviews with students, the event provided not only a fun & engaging way to strengthen Microsoft’s brand image, but also a place where students can seek career advice and industry working opportunity.

As one of 10 business groups, MSCU joined this event and demonstrated MSCU and academies’ goals, training programs, activities by multiple videos, flyers, etc. We also designed a small Mix&Match game. Students can have fun in it and quickly understand each academy functions.

More than 1,585 students went through our demo booth. The event delivered a strong “One Microsoft” image, and covered key elements driven by respective business groups.
